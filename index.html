The problem

Every major AI tool sends your data somewhere else.

Your legal strategies, financial models, and product decisions sit on US or EU servers. Regulators across UAE and KSA are tightening data residency requirements. The risk is not theoretical.

01

Data residency risk

ChatGPT, Copilot, and Gemini route queries through US and EU infrastructure. Under PDPL and emerging UAE regulations, this creates direct compliance exposure for regulated industries.

02

Generic, not company-specific

Off-the-shelf tools have no knowledge of your internal processes, your documents, your code, or your market context. Every user starts from zero, every time.

03

One size fits no team

Legal needs different outputs than Finance. Sales needs different context than Tech. A single shared AI assistant serves every team poorly instead of serving any team exceptionally.

04

Your knowledge leaves with it

Every query your team makes trains a model you do not own. Your intellectual property — your questions, your strategies, your data patterns — compound someone else's advantage.

Privacy and security

Sovereign AI. No exceptions.

ShipYard was built for organisations where data privacy is non-negotiable. Every architectural decision was made with UAE data residency requirements as the starting constraint, not an afterthought.

01

Open-source models only

No OpenAI. No Anthropic. No Microsoft. All AI inference runs on open-source models hosted on your own infrastructure inside Azure UAE North. No third-party model provider has access to your queries.

02

Client namespace isolation

Every client's knowledge base lives in a dedicated, isolated namespace. No cross-client data retrieval is architecturally possible at the infrastructure level.

03

Full audit trail

Every query, every document retrieved, every response — logged, timestamped, and exportable to your SIEM. Complete auditability for regulated industries.

04

On-prem option for enterprise

Enterprise clients who cannot accept any external dependency receive a full Docker deployment stack for air-gapped installation on their own infrastructure.
